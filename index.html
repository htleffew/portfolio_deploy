Biography

Dr. Heather Leffew is a PhD psychologist and data science executive who has spent twenty years building measurement systems that cut through ambiguity in human and machine behavior. Her career is built on a single obsession: using language, behavior, and implicit signals to produce real measurement in domains defined by ambiguity. From forensic psychiatric evaluation to billion-record platform safety at TikTok to building Spokeo's AI function from the ground up, every role has been a different implementation of the same mission: understand how humans and machines think, interact, and influence each other, and build the infrastructure to make that understanding rigorous, scalable, and actionable.

Her doctoral research at Fielding Graduate University used computational psycholinguistics to measure implicit power drives in the manifestos of mass murderers, proving that language reveals psychological states that predict behavioral outcomes even when the subject cannot be directly assessed. That work established the method she has applied ever since: take unstructured, chaotic, ambiguous data and use disciplined proxy measurement, statistical rigor, and behavioral science to extract the implicit signals that reveal what is happening beneath the surface.

At TikTok, as Principal Data Scientist and Applied Researcher, she built measurement and detection systems processing over one billion records. Her work included statistical safety frameworks for detecting semantic coercion, network analysis that shifted content moderation from chasing individual content to identifying systematic bad actors, multi-signal child safety detection, and an industry-first external IRB methodology for ethical review of global research. At Spokeo, as Director of Data Science, she built the AI function from the ground up: a 49-terabyte knowledge graph with autonomous entity resolution, a 26-research-question behavioral evaluation program, and an FCRA compliance architecture where legal requirements are machine-enforced at the inference layer.

As AI becomes increasingly integrated into the systems that judge, measure, and make decisions for humans, and as humans learn to work in partnership with machines, her work focuses on what that integration requires: understanding the states, traits, behaviors, and cognitive processes of both humans and AI systems, how they act on each other through implicit signals, and what the pathway is to using AI to improve human productivity, function, and wellness while identifying potential harms through the same implicit signals before those harms become visible in outcomes.

Her Post-Asimovian Framework for AI Alignment formalizes this as architecture: psycholinguistic typology discovery as the observable state variable for adaptive safety governance, treating alignment as a feedback control problem where linguistic features drive threshold adaptation. Her mission is to build the measurement science that the field needs to get human-AI interaction right.
