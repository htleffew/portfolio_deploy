Assessing Risk for Mass Violence From Platform Behaviors

Evaluating the statistical and conceptual limitations of utilizing unstructured, on-platform social telemetry to automate the detection of statistically rare, extreme casualty events.

Authored By Dr. Heather Leffew
Path to Intended Violence

Translating The 8 Warning Behaviors

The development of practical detection methodologies for mass violence is consistently disrupted by immense characterological heterogeneity among perpetrators. Attempting to deploy machine learning algorithms to detect these statistically rare anomalies solely through on-platform behavior (e.g. video views, static comment arrays) yields unviable false-positive densities.

This constraint is fundamentally rooted in the clinical architecture of threat escalation. True intended violence follows a progressive 6-stage lifecycle (Grievance, Ideation, Planning, Preparation, Breach, Attack), manifesting observable signals known as the 8 Essential Warning Behaviors (e.g., Leakage, Fixation, Novel Aggression). Because isolated digital behaviors typically map to the early, low-resolution stages of this pathway, purely algorithmic systems fail to contextually distinguish between explicit threats and benign, unstructured "ultimatums" designed only to relieve anxiety.
