The Survival Function of Platform Integrity
A Spark-scale Trust & Safety analytics engine built around a cross-domain reframe: the Kaplan-Meier estimator that biostatisticians use to model patient survival is the right tool to model how long a user session stays free of policy-violative content. The engine layers survival analysis, LDA topic modeling, VADER sentiment, and NetworkX co-occurrence into a four-module pipeline (orchestrator, text NLP, statistical, report generator) running on roughly 3,000 cores. The contribution is the methodological import (biostatistics to platform integrity) and the structured 12-section auto-report that makes investigations reproducible across cases.

An ADOS-2-Aligned Multimodal Architecture for Clinical Autism Assessment
The ADOS-2 scores social affect, communication, and repetitive behaviors as separate domains before deriving a composite, and a model that flattens those features into one feature space is doing something the diagnostic algorithm is built around not doing. This pipeline takes the opposite approach: three domain-specific classifiers train independently on social, communication, and repetitive feature subsets, then a Logistic Regression meta-learner combines their probability outputs into the consensus prediction. The architectural commitment carries through the output layer, which generates two Jinja-templated reports from one analytical run: a research report with AUC, calibration, and SHAP global importance for an ML team, and a clinical report with per-participant feature attribution, ensemble consensus, data-quality scoring, and explicit 'requires clinical review' flagging when uncertainty exceeds threshold. The synthetic data generator is engineered to parallel real clinical data in its complexity, overlap, ambiguity, and noise, and a runtime AUC validity check fires at 0.95 as the engineering hygiene that keeps the simulation from drifting toward separability.

Three Mechanisms in an ADHD Focus Playlist
Most 'focus' playlists hover at 70 to 90 BPM. This one runs at 145, with exaggerated stereo panning and dense polyrhythmic texture, and that is not an aesthetic choice. ADHD attention is gated by chronic under-arousal, not over-stimulation, and three audio features address the failure mode jointly: tempo at the optimal-stimulation threshold, bilateral stimulation through the vagus nerve, and structured complexity that resists habituation. The argument draws on Zentall's Optimal Stimulation Theory, the Moderate Brain Arousal model, and bilateral-stimulation research from EMDR.

LIWC and the TAT
Two paired pieces of work. A Fielding Graduate University paper makes the theoretical case that Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count belongs in Thematic Apperception Test interpretation, arguing function words carry signal about pain, depression, psychosis, and Big Five traits that content-based scoring misses. An accompanying pipeline scores 677 card responses from 69 participants across 31 TAT cards and runs a MANOVA across the five highest-frequency cards, testing whether each card pulls reliably for the themes its clinical literature claims it elicits. Card 1 pulls 1.88x corpus baseline for achievement, Card 3BM pulls 4.74x for sadness, Card 8BM pulls 4.57x for death. Every contrast reaches p < .05; most reach p < .001. The dataset is split four ways (per-response, per-participant, per-card, full corpus) to support continuous item-level scoring, where each card becomes its own measure with its own norm metric.

Implicit Power Drives in the Manifestos Preceding Autogenic Massacres
Twenty-three pre-attack manifestos from perpetrators of autogenic massacre, 111,811 author-generated words, and a single question: do these texts carry a measurable linguistic signature distinguishing their authors from non-violent writers using the same medium? Computer-mediated linguistic analysis with LIWC2015 against a 23.5-million-word comparison corpus across six baseline mediums produced a stable answer. Implicit power-drive language was elevated in the manifesto corpus relative to every baseline tested, with Welch's t-tests significant at alpha = .01 against personal blogs (t=3.47, p<.001) and expressive writing (t=3.66, p<.001), and at alpha = .05 against the multi-source composite (t=2.43, p=.012). Presented at Fielding Graduate University, Summer Session 2017, and cited as Leffew (2017) in the subsequent doctoral dissertation as the seed for that larger typological study.

Neurobiological Differentiation of Violent Offender Types
A clinical neuroscience review assessing the structural and functional neuroanatomical differences between distinct classes of violent offenders, specifically the dichotomy between impulsive/antisocial offending pathways (largely cortical in nature, marked by prefrontal regulation deficits) and instrumental/psychopathic offending (subcortical mapping, marked by amygdala hyporeactivity and reward-system anomalies). The review argues for a dimensional rather than purely categorical diagnostic approach to the two constructs, on the grounds that current categorical practice obscures the neurobiological evidence that these are different etiological pathways with different intervention efficacy profiles. The piece pairs naturally with the doctoral dissertation, which uses the same impulsive/instrumental dichotomy on the behavioral side and reaches a compatible conclusion that the two types require different threat-management responses.

Assessing Risk for Mass Violence From Platform Behaviors
Governmental studies on mass violence consistently find that most perpetrators display identifiable warning behaviors before they act (Meloy et al., 2012, identify eight: Pathway, Fixation, Identification, Novel Aggression, Energy Burst, Leakage, Directly Communicated Threat, Last Resort). Translating that clinical framework into automated platform-level detection runs into a hard wall. Perpetrators are characterologically heterogeneous, the base rate of mass violence is statistically vanishing (below 0.1%), and the early-stage warning behaviors that appear in digital traces (fixation, ideation) are also the stages where individual content looks most like the millions of users venting innocuously. The piece walks through the 8 warning behaviors mapped against the 6-stage path to intended violence (Grievance, Ideation, Planning, Preparation, Breach, Attack), with an interactive matrix showing which warning behaviors typically surface at which stages, and argues that user reports must be prioritized over algorithmic detection at the early stages because the algorithms cannot tell the difference.

Forensic Methodology for Per-User Investigation
Cohort analytics and per-user investigation are different research problems with different ethical regimes. This methodology sits on top of a Trust & Safety analytics engine and handles the cases where the unit of analysis is a single account: a six-slice temporal segmentation (Consolidated, three Tertiles, First 25, Last 25), multi-axis triangulation across sentiment / topics / view source / co-occurrence, and a structured forensic report. The methodological commitment is that risk is a trajectory rather than a snapshot. The piece is also explicit about the four guardrails (necessity, proportionality, accountability, redaction) that distinguish forensic investigation from surveillance.

Instrumental and Affective Mass Murder: A Predictive Linguistic Typology
The FBI Behavioral Analysis Unit has held since 2015 that all perpetrators of mass violence are best categorized as instrumental offenders. This dissertation rejects that position. 100 pre-attack communications analyzed with LIWC2015 along four summary dimensions; K-means clustering separated the corpus into two psychometrically distinct types, Affective (n=53) and Instrumental (n=47), with linear discriminant analysis correctly classifying 100% of cases. A binary logistic regression on four refined attack-behavior variables recovered cluster membership at McFadden R² = 0.24, with perpetrator-killed-by-another carrying an odds ratio of 14.11. The contribution is the first within-groups typology of mass murderers with statistically validated predictive ties to specific attack characteristics.
